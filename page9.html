About 3D Printing and Scanning

3D scanning can be used to render models in 3D modelling software such as Google Sketchup or Solidworks. The scan picks up most details, which can then be finalised or edited in modelling software. This is quicker than dafting an object from scratch 

Test Scan

This image shows a test scan of a toy pumpkin - The render is achieved by scanning the object from all sides at a constant distance. This creates a 3 dimensional silhouette which can converted into a scan.

Undertaking my own test scan

Upon starting the Structure.io application, I selected the start new scan option. I then circumnavigated a banana and orange balanced on top of each other from a constant distance. My laptop can not open the scan file, but here is the thumbnail I received of the scan. A problem with this scanning method is that it is difficult to capture the underside of the objects scanned, especially if they curve inwards.
