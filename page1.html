FUSION 360

FUSION 360 is a user friendly CAD software which can be used to produce accurate and easily sharable technical drawings. Below is a step by step guide of how to model a die using FUSION 360

FUSION 360 WEBSITE

Step 1

Enter sketch mode by selecting create sketch in the tool bar, align your axes and select the rectangle tool.

Step 2

Once you have selected the rectangle tool, enter in your parameters and hit 'Enter'

Step 3

Select your square, and in the sketch toolbar choose the 'fillet' option.

Step 4

Select the relevant corners of the square to fillet, and the fillet radii - press 'enter' when done.

Step 4

Select the square again, and this time choose the extrude tool.

Step 5

Select the correct extrude parameters (10mm) and hit 'Enter'.

Step 6

Now select all the new edges of the cube that you want to fillet, and choose the fillet tool under the modify toolbar.

Step 7

Choose the same radius as when previously filleting the corners of the square, hit 'Enter'. 

Step 8

Now from the construct toolbar, choose the 'Offset Plane' option. This allows you to create a reference plane from which you can position other shapes and objects.

Step 9

Use the offset panel to select the positioning of the new plane in reference to another face or axis.

Step 10

By adding new planes, it becomes easier to add features to the die.

Step 11

Selecting a plane which sits in line with one of the cube's faces, choose the circle tool from the sketch panel, and using the automatic guides draw a circle in the centre of the face.

Step 12

Select the circle and choose the rectangular pattern option from the sketch panel.

Step 13

Ensure that the rectangular pattern box is filled out in a way which corresponds to the pattern which you want on the die.

Step 14

 Select your pattern of circles (all 6 circles in this case) and then choose the extrude tool. 

Step 15

By adding more reference planes, to add extruded circle patterns, the die will start to take shape.
