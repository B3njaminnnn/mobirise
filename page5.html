About Laser Cutting

Laser cutting uses a highly powerful laser to burn through a variety of materials such as woods, plastics, metals, card and even glass. The strength and travel speed of the laser determines whether it cuts or engraves. It is important to note that different laser travel speed and intensity are needed depending on the material used, and its thickness. Operating the settings depends on the laser cutter used, but finding the necessary settings for the material used is very easy to do using the internet.

LASER CUTTING A SLOT MODEL

STEP 1

Before laser cutting, you need to create a file to laser cut. There are a variety of programs you can use to do this, such as 2D Design and Adobe Illustrator. I used the latter. 

The laser cutter will only cut if the line used is RGB red. On the right you can see correct colour code to use when laser cutting. 



STEP 2

I wanted to create a slot model of a spherical object. Using illustrator I drafted all the pieces I needed to slot together to create the model.

However, before cutting out I needed to run a sample to see what line thickness was needed to ensure that the slots would friction fit together.

STEP 3

The graphic on the left is a tolerance test. It explores different slot thicknesses which can then be cut out and slotted together in different configurations to determine which size of slot is best for a good friction fit.

STEP 4

Before starting the test laser cut, it is necessary to ensure the coordinates of the laser sit in the right position. This is achieved by touching the 'x/y' button and then moving the laser gently into the right position gently by hand.

Once this is done, firm the position by pressing 'Set Home'

You also may need to use the magnetic distance gauge (shown in the image) to position the laser at the right distance from the bed. The bed can be raised and lowered using the up and down arrows shown on the control pad.

N.B: Photo of Pad controls shown in 2 steps

STEP 5

Before loading up the sketch, it is important to turn on BOTH extractors for the laser cutter. Otherwise it will not be fumigated which could allow inhalation of toxic fumes.

STEP 6

Once you have selected the relevant cut parameters using the laser cutting software, then you can select the print option. The file should be visible on the laser cutter control panel, after JOB: . At this point you should press Go, and survey your cutting job.
